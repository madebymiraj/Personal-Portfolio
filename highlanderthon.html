About the Project

HighlanderThon, a student-run organization, strives to provide financial and emotional support for the children and families treated at Children's Specialized Hospital through shared experiences that foster a significant bond within our community.

Identifying the Problem

Discovering the Core Values of the Organization

Why?

  • To provide financial and emotional support for the children and families at our local children's hospital, Children's Specialized Hospital.
  • To unite the school community with one shared cause.
  • To build and increase school spirit and morale at NJIT.

How?

  • By making fundraising a fun, competitive effort, and providing resources and workshops to train participants how to fundraise.
  • By actively spreading awareness, fundraising for donations, and seeking sponsorships to help support the cause.
  • As an organization: staying united, selfless, accountable, hyped, and limitless.

What?

  • We host events throughout the year leading up to an 18 hour long Dance Marathon with live music and entertainment.

Understanding Our Users

Identifying the Various Stakeholders We are Targeting

Our strategies were grounded in a deep understanding of our users to help us effectively deliver a great experience by making them the center of attention during every step of the process. Our insights were based what our users need, how they think, and why they behave the way they do. We discovered that our users consisted of four major users groups: participants, volunteers, donors, and sponsors.

Participants

Undergraduate students at the New Jersey Institute of Technology between the ages of 17 to 25, who are usually identified as geeky/nerdy in nature and often lack school spirit.

Volunteers

Students at the New Jersey Institute of Technology who enjoy community service/helping out, or are members of the Honors college and need to fulfill requirements for volunteering hours.

Donors

Users of all ages from any location worldwide that are referred by other stakeholders, usually participants, to make donations either through the website or in person.

Sponsors

Small to large scale local businesses that desire to benefit their business by supporting the cause either through capital or by sponsoring through relevant commodities.

Aligning Our Vision

Driving Actions Through a Shared Purpose

A Collaborative Moodboard

A moodboard is a compilation of various types of media which adhere to certain emotions, which we felt were relevant to our events. We shared pictures, music, videos, websites, articles, anything that helped demonstrate what our users would experience, mostly focusing on our partipants and volunteers.

We understood that every decision we make would be crucial to HighlanderThon for years to come as we set precedents to the first ever. We held the power to dictate our actions to make our vision for the event to come true. To help us come to a general consesus on our vision, I conducted a fun exercise with my public relations committee and the executive team to work collaboratively on a moodboard.

The moodboard allowed us to not only align our vision, but to also visualize and get a feel for it. It hinted a highly energetic, vibrant experience that would spark something within our community. From which, we came up with our slogan, "Ignite the Hype." It also contributed in helping us pick out our colors and typography to get us started on branding HighlanderThon.
